My name is Pedro Brunialti Lima de Andrade (he/him), and I'm a fourth-year Mathematics PhD student at the University of Virginia working under Julie Bergner. Before that, I did my undergrad in physics and my master's in mathematics at the Federal University of Minas Gerais.

I am interested in (\(\infty\),n)-categories, more specifically how they can be applied to TQFTs. A big part of my research is understanding the kind of extra structure that (\(\infty\),n)-categories of bordisms have. I am also also interested in equivariant homotopy theory and equivariant algebra; I am studying the analogs of some classical algebraic notions for Mackey and Tambara functors, such as free and projective modules.
